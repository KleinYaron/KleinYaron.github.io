About

Operator first, with investing experience. Built to be useful in product, strategy, and early-stage environments.

I started in strategy at KPMG, moved into venture at OurCrowd, and then joined IronVest to work closer to product creation. That path gave me two lenses I still use every day: how to evaluate an opportunity, and how hard it is to actually build one.

At IronVest, I worked across product, growth, and operations on AI and cybersecurity products. I helped launch and scale an AI security product to $1M ARR and 10K+ paying users, ran discovery and experimentation, wrote product requirements, and partnered across engineering, GTM, and leadership.

Today I am an MBA candidate at Cornell Johnson, active in Big Red Ventures, and building practical AI products and prototypes. I am most energized by roles that sit at the intersection of user insight, technical judgment, and strategic decision-making.

Investment Thinking

Included to make my venture interest concrete, not just implied.

What I look for

I am most drawn to products solving painful, repeated workflows where urgency is obvious, value can be measured quickly, and the wedge can deepen over time through data, trust, or workflow lock-in.

Sectors I track

AI infrastructure, cybersecurity, digital identity, and software for regulated or high-stakes environments. I like markets where technical capability matters, but distribution, trust, and product discipline matter just as much.

How operating experience shapes my lens

Working inside a startup changed how I evaluate products. I pay attention to implementation friction, user behavior, GTM realism, and how teams prioritize under constraints, not just how strong the deck looks.

How I can help

I can be useful where product judgment, structured analysis, and founder empathy overlap: market mapping, customer pain framing, product diligence, roadmap thinking, or special projects close to the leadership team.
